# Ainu Ritual & Cosmology Traditions Registry

This repository preserves, in an immutable and decentralized format, the ritual and cosmological traditions of the Ainu — the Indigenous people of northern Japan (Hokkaidō) and Sakhalin. Ainu cosmology is one of the most sophisticated animistic systems in East Asia, centered on reciprocity between humans and kamuy (spirits).

The registry documents major rituals such as **iomante** (bear sending ceremony), **inau offerings**, **fire rituals dedicated to Kamuy Fuchi**, seasonal hunting rites, salmon return ceremonies, and household spiritual practices.

## Why Ainu Cosmology Matters

Ainu ritual life is:

- a **reciprocal relationship** with kamuy (spirits who visit the human world)  
- a **seasonal cosmology** tied to salmon runs, hunting cycles, and ecological rhythms  
- a **ritual technology** involving carved inau, libations, chants, and dances  
- a **spiritual grammar** encoded in offerings, gestures, and ceremonial objects  
- a **living philosophy** of respect, gratitude, and interdependence  

Rituals like *iomante* are not sacrifices but **acts of returning a visiting spirit to its realm with honor**.

## Historical Suppression

During the Meiji period, the Japanese state:

- banned Ainu rituals  
- criminalized iomante  
- confiscated ceremonial objects  
- destroyed nusa (altar poles)  
- prohibited inau carving  
- suppressed Ainu language and oral tradition  

Missionaries further targeted Ainu cosmology as “pagan,” accelerating cultural loss.

Documenting these traditions on-chain is an act of **cultural vindication**, ensuring they cannot be erased again.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, censorship-resistant record** of Ainu ritual traditions.  
2. **Preserve Indigenous knowledge** that was nearly eradicated by state policy.  
3. **Document rituals with precision**: materials, procedures, symbolism, and cosmology.  
4. **Provide an immutable reference** for museums, researchers, and future generations.  
5. **Honor the spiritual worldview of the Ainu people.**

The contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no monetization, no barriers  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls  
- **permanent** — entries become part of the cultural ledger  

## What Each Entry Records

- **ritualName** — iomante, inau offerings, fire rituals, seasonal rites  
- **spiritualEntities** — kamuy involved in the ritual  
- **materials** — inau, sake, carved objects, ceremonial tools  
- **procedures** — ritual steps, chants, dances, offerings  
- **symbolism** — meaning within Ainu cosmology  
- **culturalContext** — seasonal cycles, clan roles, social meaning
